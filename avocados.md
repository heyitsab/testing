## 🌿 General

- 🌳 Avocados are the fruit of the *Persea americana* tree, native to south-central Mexico.
- 🫐 They are botanically classified as a **large berry** containing a single seed — yes, a BERRY! 🤯
- 👑 The most popular variety is the **Hass avocado**, which accounts for over 80% of avocados eaten worldwide.
- ♾️ Avocado trees are evergreen and can live for **hundreds of years** — true legends of the plant world!

## 💪 Nutrition

```

  |  🟢 Healthy Fats      |
  |  🍌 More K than banana|
  |  🌾 Fiber champion    |
  |  💊 20 vitamins!      |
  +-----------------------+
```

- 🫒 Avocados are rich in **healthy monounsaturated fats**, particularly oleic acid.
- 💊 A single avocado contains about **20 different vitamins and minerals** — it's basically a multivitamin!
- ⚡ They are an excellent source of **potassium** — containing more potassium per gram than bananas 🍌 (sorry, bananas).
- 🌾 Avocados are high in **fiber**, with roughly 7 grams per half fruit.
- 🔬 They contain vitamins K, C, B5, B6, and folate (B9).
- 🚫 Despite being high in fat, avocados are **cholesterol- and sodium-free**. Get rekt, bad fats!

## 🏛️ History & Culture

- 📜 Avocados have been cultivated in Central and South America for at least **5,000 years** — they were ancient before ancient was cool.
- 🗣️ The word "avocado" comes from the Nahuatl word *āhuacatl*.
- ⛵ Spanish conquistadors documented avocados in the 16th century and introduced them to Europe.
- 🇺🇸 They were introduced to the United States in the early 19th century — and the rest is brunch history.

## 🌱 Growing

```
   🌱 → 🌿 → 🌳 → 🥑🥑🥑
  seed  sprout  tree  PROFIT
```

- ☀️ Avocado trees require a warm climate and thrive in Mexico 🇲🇽, Peru 🇵🇪, Indonesia 🇮🇩, and California 🏄.
- 🏆 Mexico is the world's largest producer, supplying roughly **30% of global production** — absolute legends.
- 🔢 A single avocado tree can produce around **200–300 fruits per year**. That's a lot of toast! 🍞
- ⏳ It takes a newly planted tree **3–5 years** to bear fruit. Patience, young avocado farmer.
- 😮 Avocados do **not** ripen on the tree — they only ripen after being harvested. Drama queens! 💅

- ☠️ Avocados are **toxic to many animals**, including birds 🐦, rabbits 🐇, and cats 🐱 — keep it away from your furry pals!
- 📬 The Hass avocado was discovered by a **mail carrier** named Rudolph Hass, who patented the tree in 1935. A postal worker changed guacamole history. 🫡
- 🌮 Avocado is a key ingredient in **guacamole**, a dish with pre-Columbian origins. It's been a party staple for millennia!
- 🖊️ The inside of an avocado pit can be used as a **natural ink or dye** — ancient highlighter, anyone?
- 🐊 Avocados are sometimes called "**alligator pears**" because of their bumpy green skin. Cutest alligator ever! 🥰
