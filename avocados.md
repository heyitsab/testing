# 10 Interesting Facts About Avocados

1. **Avocados are technically a fruit** — More specifically, they are a single-seeded berry. Despite being used in savory dishes, they are botanically classified as a fruit.

2. **They are native to Central America** — Avocados have been cultivated in Mexico and Central America for at least 5,000 years. The Aztecs called them "ahuacatl."

3. **Avocados don't ripen on the tree** — They only ripen after being picked. The tree acts as a natural storage unit, and farmers can leave avocados on the tree for months to delay harvest.

4. **They are packed with healthy fats** — Avocados are rich in monounsaturated fats, particularly oleic acid, the same beneficial fat found in olive oil. These fats can help reduce inflammation and lower bad cholesterol.

5. **Avocados have more potassium than bananas** — A 100-gram serving of avocado contains about 485 mg of potassium, while the same amount of banana contains about 358 mg.

6. **The Hass variety dominates the market** — Roughly 80% of all avocados sold worldwide are the Hass variety, which originated from a single tree planted by Rudolph Hass in California in 1926.

7. **Avocados are a complete food** — They contain nearly 20 vitamins and minerals, including vitamins K, C, B5, B6, and E, as well as folate and magnesium.

8. **They were once considered a luxury food** — In the early 20th century, avocados were marketed as "alligator pears" and were so expensive that restaurants advertised them as luxury items to attract wealthy diners.

9. **Avocado trees need a partner to produce fruit** — Avocado flowers have male and female phases that open at different times. For optimal fruit production, two trees of different varieties are typically planted together for cross-pollination.

10. **Exposure to air turns avocados brown quickly** — Avocados contain an enzyme called polyphenol oxidase that reacts with oxygen, causing browning. Squeezing lemon or lime juice on the flesh can slow this process by reducing oxidation.
