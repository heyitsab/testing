# Alpaca Fun Facts 🦙

1. **Two species exist** – There are two breeds of alpaca: the Huacaya (fluffy and teddy bear-like) and the Suri (with long, silky, dreadlock-like fiber).

2. **Native to South America** – Alpacas are native to the Andes mountains of Peru, Bolivia, and Chile, where they have been domesticated for thousands of years.

3. **Super soft fiber** – Alpaca fiber is softer than cashmere, hypoallergenic, and naturally water-resistant, making it highly prized in the textile industry.

4. **They hum** – Alpacas communicate through a variety of sounds, most commonly a soft humming noise that expresses contentment, curiosity, or concern.

5. **No top front teeth** – Like other camelids, alpacas have no upper front teeth. They use a dental pad on their upper jaw to grind food.

6. **Communal dung piles** – Alpacas are very tidy animals. They use communal dung piles, which makes cleaning their enclosures much easier.

7. **Long lifespan** – Alpacas typically live between 15 and 20 years.

8. **Eco-friendly grazers** – Their soft, padded feet are gentle on pastures, and they only eat the tops of grass rather than pulling it up by the roots.

9. **Spitting is a warning** – Alpacas spit mostly at each other to establish hierarchy or when they feel threatened — not usually at people unless provoked.

10. **Crias are born ready** – Baby alpacas, called *crias*, are typically born in the morning and can stand and walk within just a few hours of birth.
