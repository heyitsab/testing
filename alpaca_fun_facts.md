# Alpaca Fun Facts 🦙

## General Facts 🌎

- **Alpacas are domesticated animals** from South America, primarily found in Peru 🇵🇪, Bolivia 🇧🇴, and Chile 🇨🇱.
- They are part of the camelid family, along with llamas, camels 🐪, guanacos, and vicuñas.
- Alpacas were domesticated over 6,000 years ago by the ancient Incas 🏛️.

## Physical Characteristics 📏

- **Two types exist**: Huacaya (fluffy, crimped fleece) ☁️ and Suri (silky, dreadlock-like fleece) ✨.
- Alpacas typically weigh between 100-200 pounds (45-90 kg) ⚖️.
- They stand about 3 feet tall at the shoulder (90 cm) 📐.
- Their lifespan is approximately 15-20 years ⏳.

## Behavior & Personality 💖

- Alpacas are **gentle and intelligent** animals 🧠.
- They communicate through body language and humming sounds 🎵.
- They're naturally curious 🤔 but can be shy around strangers.
- Alpacas are herd animals and prefer living in groups 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦.

## Wool & Fiber 🧶

- Alpaca fiber is **softer than cashmere** and warmer than sheep's wool 🐑.
- They produce about 5-10 pounds of fiber per year 📦.
- Their fleece comes in 22 natural colors, from white to black 🎨.
- Alpaca fiber is hypoallergenic and doesn't contain lanolin ✅.

## Diet & Lifestyle 🌱

- Alpacas are herbivores and mainly eat grass 🌾 and hay.
- They consume about 2% of their body weight in food daily 🍽️.
- Unlike other grazing animals, they have soft padded feet 🦶 that don't damage pastures.
- They designate communal dung piles to keep their living areas clean 🧹.

## Interesting Tidbits ⭐

- Alpacas don't have top front teeth 🦷 - they have a dental pad instead.
- They can live comfortably at altitudes over 15,000 feet (4,500 m) ⛰️.
- When upset or threatened, alpacas may spit 💦 (but usually only at each other).
- Baby alpacas are called "crias" 👶 and can walk within an hour of being born.
- Alpacas have excellent eyesight 👀 and can see 360 degrees around them.

## Conservation 🌿

- While alpacas are domesticated and not endangered, their wild relatives (vicuñas) were once critically endangered 🚨.
- Today, there are approximately 3-4 million alpacas worldwide 🌍.
- Most of the world's alpacas still live in South America 🗺️.
