# Clever Jokes

A curated collection of witty, clever jokes for the discerning reader.

---

## Programming & Logic

- **Why do programmers prefer dark mode?**
  Because light attracts bugs.

- **A SQL query walks into a bar, walks up to two tables and asks...**
  "Can I join you?"

- **Why do Java developers wear glasses?**
  Because they don't C#.

- **There are 10 types of people in the world:**
  Those who understand binary, and those who don't.

- **A programmer's partner says: "Go to the store, get a gallon of milk, and if they have eggs, get a dozen."**
  The programmer comes home with 12 gallons of milk.

## Science & Math

- **Why can't you trust an atom?**
  Because they make up everything.

- **A photon checks into a hotel. The bellhop asks, "Can I help you with your luggage?"**
  The photon replies, "No thanks, I'm traveling light."

- **Schrödinger's cat walks into a bar. And doesn't.**

- **An infinite number of mathematicians walk into a bar.**
  The first orders 1 beer. The second orders 1/2 a beer. The third orders 1/4 a beer.
  The bartender pours two beers and says, "Know your limits."

- **What do you call two crows on a branch?**
  Attempted murder. *(It only counts as murder if there are three or more.)*

## Philosophy & Wordplay

- **I told my doctor I broke my arm in two places.**
  She told me to stop going to those places.

- **The past, present, and future walked into a bar.**
  It was tense.

- **I asked the librarian if they had books about paranoia.**
  She whispered, "They're right behind you."

- **A pessimist and an optimist die and go to the same place.**
  The pessimist says, "It could be worse."
  The optimist says, "No, it really couldn't."

- **Why is it hard to explain puns to kleptomaniacs?**
  They always take things literally.

## Classic Wit

- **I'm reading a book about anti-gravity.**
  It's impossible to put down.

- **I used to hate facial hair, but then it grew on me.**

- **The invention of the shovel was truly groundbreaking.**

- **I asked my dog what two minus two is.**
  He said nothing.

- **Why don't scientists trust atoms?**
  Because they make up everything — *and they've been doing it for 13.8 billion years.*
