# Sonnet of the Coqui

Beneath the canopy of Puerto Rico's night,
A tiny frog lifts up its golden song,
*Co-quí, co-quí* — a call both pure and bright,
That echoes through the rainforest all night long.

No bigger than a thumbnail, yet its voice
Rings out above the rustle of the trees,
Among the chorus it has made its choice
To serenade the warm and tropic breeze.

Its amber eyes behold the moonlit ferns,
It clings to leaf and bark with tender grace,
And as the humid darkness slowly turns,
It fills the dark with song and tiny face.

  O coqui, symbol of a proud isle's heart —
  Small, fierce, and sweet, a world-class work of art.
